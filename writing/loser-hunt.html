by Ben Levy
Written during English Literature studies, Ben-Gurion University

I was at work when I received the email. It said, "You have been chosen to take part in our journey to become messengers of the law," or something along those fancy lines. For a moment it felt like a good thing. I finally was presented with the opportunity of becoming a man. But it was far. Miles away, which in other circumstances would be awesome, yet the reality was real. I had to announce it to my mother. I had to have her blessing.

She was sitting in the living room watching The Big Brother, her favorite TV show, eating Tapuchips, and drinking Yarden Cabernet Sauvignon. Subtly, I opened the door, she didn't notice my presence. My bare feet were glued to the ground; each step was a nightmare. I felt sick, something about thinking of how to bring up the words brought up something else. "Mama," I said. "Yes darling." "I bear some news." "Well let's hear them. Are they pleasant news?" she said with slight excitement. "Are you finally getting this promotion that you desired?" Ultimately, it was a promotion, just not the kind she was hoping for. "No mama. It isn't that," I mumbled. "So, speak up! What is it?" "I received an email. I was elected to this prestigious school." She grinned. I saw her calculating the various possibilities arising from this opportunity. Hope filled me up. I could see myself wandering among those halls, smiling, and waving my hands to those people who know me and appreciate me. The black cloak will fit me well, I can see it now. But then she asked this horrifying question. "Where is this school, honey?" I froze. "Don't be mad Mama, but it is kinda far." "Far?" She panicked. Something about her tone implied that this cloak wouldn't fit me so well, and those people are never going to know me. She finally switched the topic. Redemption, I thought. It was too soon to think that, because she started by saying "I was at the doctor's yesterday."

She tended to start to talk about her medical problems in these kinds of moments. She believed that it would make me stay. She believed that my love for her and my conscience will make me stay. And she was right. I always stayed. I have turned down countless job and study applications. Some of them crushed my heart; it's difficult to take your own dreams and take a huge dump on them all. And somehow, I kept sending those applications, hoping that one day she'll understand. Yet, there she was. "The doctor said that the tumor in my leg is actually in a severe potential of becoming malignant," tears were pooling in the corners of her eyes. Every day she had a new potentially malignant tumor and new revelation regarding her illnesses. "Honey, he says that I might die soon." My heart sank. Maybe this time it is dangerous, I thought. I didn't know, and I didn't want to lose her. And if so, and she was destined to die, I wanted to be near her at all times. "Mama, if it is malignant, how much time do you have left?" I asked, shivering. "Honey, don't worry, he said that if it is the case, most likely that I'll have to amputate. And you are here to help me through it, aren't you?" "Yes, I am, mama." Once again, I had to let go of my ambitions and stay here. To stay at this lousy job in the supermarket in this shitty kibbutz. Yes, she needed me more than ever, but this time I had a feeling that it'll be different.

Life in kibbutz Revivim is rather uninteresting, and unless you're Daniel, it's hardly a place to grow into a scholar or a great businessman. Daniel was my sole (and best) friend. He was a very accomplished and affluent man, and I approached him whenever I needed help or advice, like this time. So, I stepped outdoors for some fresh air and texted him.

I usually stay away from trouble. That's why in high school Daniel and I weren't exactly best friends. So how has it changed you might ask? I actually don't know. One day he appeared on my doorstep with a wide grin, asking to use the bathroom. He had this charm. Even in high school, he was incredibly popular, dated the prettiest girl, and was the soccer team's star player. He just knew how to talk everyone into doing what he wanted. I guess it explains why I didn't like him then. He appeared to be so phony, and I didn't exactly live the lifestyle he did. But on that particular day at my house, his smile and manner of speaking won me over. We quickly became best friends. Me and Daniel are best buddies. I, who was this shy insecure little kid, am friends with the most popular kid in school. Who would have imagined? So, it is understandable why despite my moral code I did as he said and searched for some information about this Deep Web. After reading and doing small research online I realized that in order to get a real sense of what it is, I have to experience it firsthand. After all, it is deep inside the internet because it contains illegal stuff, so reading about it on the regular internet is kind of pointless. I downloaded the TOR browser. At first, it seemed pretty regular, maybe some pot selling and prostitution but nothing radical. After a few hours of surfing this Deep Web, I decided to go to bed. But then a message popped.

3$O999: OPEN

I unconsciously clicked.

Suddenly many windows appeared and when I say many, I mean loads of them. Countless forums on topics that I didn't even know existed. From "How to use 'Special K'", which is apparently Ketamine, a common kind of roofie, to "Recipes and Meat supplies." As a carnivore myself I decided to take a look. New meat recipes are a no-brainer for me. Meat is kind of an addiction of mine. Sometimes in the supermarket when we get new cuts of meat, I like to organize them myself. Just thinking about them cooking and sizzling on the grill makes me drool. Man what I would do for a nice Prime Rib steak, I thought. Well, very quickly I discovered that this is not the kind of meat I tend to cook. The prices were very high; $1,000 for breasts is just outrageous, I thought. Then I clicked on an image link that said, "Thank you." I immediately threw up. It was a selfie of a bearded sweaty old man with shiny eyes, devouring a human hand. He wrote: "Thank you GINGER6MEAT for this delicious piece." He even attached a recipe.

  1. Drain the blood into a ball. (Keep it, you'll need it).
  2. Take the skin off — salt it — and put it in the oven for dehydration.
  3. Now make a marinade out of blood, soy sauce, garlic, and chopped fingernails.
  4. Put the whole hand inside…

Cannibals! I got sick again. Who are those people?! Why would someone do it and pay so much money for this kind of crap?! And most importantly, how did they get those meat cuts?! They might have chopped it off fresh corpses, slaughtered people for it, or even severed a living person to keep it young. The possibilities kept rising in my mind. I quickly closed up this window. It was then that I noticed all of those forums with people's names in their titles. So many names. I didn't expect anything, yet curiosity got the better of me, and I searched for my name.

There it was. A forum with my name. At first, I thought it might be related to someone else with my name, but the pictures and the description convinced me otherwise. I didn't have anything left to vomit. Pictures of me, from the age of 7 till now. Lots of them. My work at the supermarket, my family, and even my wish list which included my dream of leaving home and becoming a lawyer, everything was there. Tears filled up my eyes. The forum had about 130 users, and all of them were active. Mean words, so many mean and ominous words. "Loser, hopeless, pathetic, hideous, dumb, miserable, why does he think that he has a future XD," and many more, mean mean words. So many folders, so many years. Somebody was documenting my life and uploading it to the Deep Web. Money bars everywhere. I was shivering. People bet my life decisions.
